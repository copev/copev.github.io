Minimal Weather App

The Minimal Weather App project explores the use of simplfied yet expressive shapes and information to inspire a functional wethaer application focused on aesthetic design. Weather applications can range from overbearingly complex in information visualization, to simplified views that place visual design as a lower priority.

This fun, personal project was another way to explore Gestalt principles in a tangible way and focus on key weather states to convey information visually with only vital supporting data. In this project, the design choices are inspired by a physical place and climate to improve referential skills and relationships in design choices.

The chosen color palette and certain weather states are inspired by the caribbean island St. Lucia, home to a warm, tropical climate tempered by trade winds throughout most of the year.
